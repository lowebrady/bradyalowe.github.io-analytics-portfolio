05 — A/B Testing / Marketing Analytics

Allbirds A/B
Email Campaign Analysis

Conducted and analyzed an A/B test on promotional email subject lines for a seasonal marketing campaign — evaluating open rates, click-through rates, and conversions to deliver data-backed recommendations for improving customer engagement.

Role Marketing Analyst
Tools Excel, Statistical Analysis
Method A/B Testing, Hypothesis Testing
Context MSU Milestone Project — Allbirds Case
01 — The Business Problem

Email is still the highest-ROI channel — if you get the subject line right

For DTC (direct-to-consumer) brands like Allbirds, email marketing remains one of the highest-ROI channels available. But most email performance lives or dies on one decision: the subject line. It determines whether the email is opened at all — and an email that isn't opened can't convert.

The question for this project: between two subject line strategies — product-focused vs. value/benefit-focused — which drives better performance across the full funnel? Not just opens, but clicks and conversions too.

"Open rate is a vanity metric if it doesn't connect to revenue. A great subject line that opens but doesn't click-through is just a broken promise to the customer."

02 — Test Design

Setting up an experiment that actually answers the question

Good A/B testing requires rigorous setup before a single email is sent. Poorly designed tests produce results that look conclusive but aren't.

  • 01
    Hypothesis Formulation H₀: There is no significant difference in open rate between subject line A (product-focused: "Meet Our New Natural Wool Runner") and subject line B (benefit-focused: "Your Most Comfortable Run Yet"). H₁: Subject line B produces a higher open rate.
  • 02
    Sample Size & Randomization Calculated the required sample size to achieve 80% statistical power at a 95% confidence level given the expected baseline open rate. Confirmed random assignment to control and treatment groups to eliminate selection bias.
  • 03
    Metrics Definition Primary metric: open rate. Secondary metrics: click-through rate (CTR) and conversion rate (purchase within 48 hours of email open). Defined all metrics in advance to prevent p-hacking after results came in.
  • 04
    Statistical Analysis in Excel Used Excel's T.TEST() function to calculate p-values for the difference in open rates between variants. Built a results summary table with confidence intervals around each metric estimate.
03 — Results

What the test revealed

+14%

Open Rate Lift (Variant B)

The benefit-focused subject line outperformed the product-focused variant by 14 percentage points in open rate — a statistically significant result at p < 0.05.

+8%

CTR Lift (Variant B)

Higher open rates translated downstream — variant B also drove an 8% higher click-through rate, confirming that the subject line set better expectations for the email content.

p < 0.05

Statistically Significant

The open rate difference cleared the pre-defined significance threshold. Results are not attributable to random variation with 95% confidence.

+22%

Estimated Revenue Lift

Projecting the CTR improvement across Allbirds' email list size suggests a meaningful revenue lift per campaign send — making the subject line choice a high-leverage decision.

An important nuance: while the benefit-focused subject line won on open rate and CTR, the conversion rate gap was smaller and did not reach statistical significance with the available sample. This suggests the product-focused variant, while generating fewer opens, may attract a more purchase-intent audience. A follow-up test with a larger sample would be needed to resolve this.

04 — Recommendations

Actionable guidance with appropriate caveats

  • ↳
    Default to benefit-focused subject lines for awareness-stage campaigns The open rate advantage is clear and significant. For campaigns targeting cold or lapsed audiences, benefit framing wins because it speaks to customer outcomes rather than product features most recipients don't yet know.
  • ↳
    Consider product-focused lines for high-intent segments The conversion rate data, while inconclusive, hints that product-focused lines may self-select for more purchase-ready openers. Test this hypothesis on segmented lists of past purchasers or cart abandoners.
  • ↳
    Run a follow-up test with 2× sample size to resolve the conversion question The most important unanswered question is whether the open rate advantage translates to incremental revenue. A larger test with conversion as the primary metric would answer this definitively.
  • ↳
    Build a subject line testing cadence into the marketing calendar One test is a data point. Ten tests across segments and seasons builds a body of evidence that compounds. Systematizing A/B testing turns a one-time insight into an ongoing competitive advantage.
